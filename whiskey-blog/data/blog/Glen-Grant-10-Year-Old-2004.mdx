        # Glen Grant 10 Year Old Review

**Whisky Name:** Glen Grant 10 Year Old  
**Brand:** Glen Grant  
**Price:** £36.84  
**ABV:** 40%

Welcome to the world of Scotch whiskies, where every sip tells a story, and today's tale comes from the Glen Grant distillery with their 10 Year Old single malt. Let's uncork this bottle and dive into the experience.

## First Impressions

Upon pouring, you're greeted with a golden hue that's reminiscent of autumn afternoons. The bottle design is unassuming, yet it hints at the tradition and heritage that Glen Grant carries with its name. It's a classic Speyside distillery, known for producing whiskies that are approachable and often recommended to those new to Scotch.

## The Flavour Journey

Nosing this whisky, one might detect a bouquet of floral notes, intertwined with a subtle sweetness of ripe orchard fruits—think apples and pears. There's a whisper of almond and vanilla, likely a gentle nod to its time spent in oak barrels.

The first sip confirms the nose's promise, delivering a smooth, light to medium-bodied palate. It's a dance of sweetness and a touch of spice, with the oak's influence more pronounced here, lending a touch of dryness that balances the initial sweetness.

The finish is moderately long, warm, and slightly dry, leaving a pleasant, lingering taste of honey and a hint of spice. It's a straightforward, no-nonsense dram that doesn't overcomplicate things.

## Value for Money

At just under £40, Glen Grant 10 Year Old positions itself as a solid entry-level single malt. It's not going to blow your mind with complexity, but it doesn't need to. For its price point, it offers consistency and a pleasurable sipping experience that won't intimidate newcomers nor bore the seasoned enthusiasts.

## Similar Whiskies

If you find yourself enjoying Glen Grant 10 Year Old, you might also appreciate whiskies like the Glenfiddich 12 Year Old or the Glenlivet Founder's Reserve. Both are similarly priced and offer that characteristic Speyside fruitiness with their own unique twists.

## The Distillery and Brand

Glen Grant has a storied history dating back to 1840, and it's been a staple in the Speyside region of Scotland. They're known for a distinctive style that often emphasizes light, crisp, and fruit-forward flavors, and this 10 Year Old expression is a testament to their craft.

## Final Thoughts

**Rating:** 3.5/5

In summary, the Glen Grant 10 Year Old is a whisky that delivers exactly what it promises—a pleasant, easy-drinking Scotch with enough character to keep you coming back for more. It's a great introductory malt for those starting their whisky journey and a reliable choice for a casual evening dram. It's not the most complex or bold whisky out there, but at this price point, it's a solid and enjoyable dram that offers good value for money.
