        # The Antiquary 21 Year Old Whisky Review

**ABV:** 43%  
**Price:** $84.90

Step into the world of aged Scotch with The Antiquary 21 Year Old, a blend that promises to offer more than just a sip of whisky, but a journey through time and tradition.

## First Impressions

Upon first glance, The Antiquary 21 Year Old holds an inviting golden amber color that suggests a richness and depth waiting to be explored. The presentation is classic and understated, much like the whisky itself. The bottle design, with its unique faceted shape, speaks to the heritage of the brand and the care put into this aged blend.

## Flavor Notes

Bringing the glass to your nose, you are greeted with a bouquet of aromas that intermingle gracefully. Notes of ripe fruits, perhaps a dash of orange zest, and a whisper of almond dance around the more robust scents of oak and a hint of smokiness. It's a complex aroma that invites you to take your first sip with anticipation.

Upon tasting, The Antiquary 21 Year Old reveals its mature character. A smooth, velvety texture coats the palate, delivering flavors that mirror the nose with remarkable fidelity. There's a sweetness there, reminiscent of toffee or caramel, balanced by a subtle spiciness and the warmth of oak. The finish is long and satisfying, with a gentle smokiness that lingers, reminding you of the whisky's age and refinement.

## Value for Money

At a price point of just under $85, The Antiquary 21 Year Old positions itself as a reasonably priced option for a whisky of its age. It's a blend that doesn't shout for attention but instead rewards the drinker with a consistent and enjoyable experience. For those looking to explore aged blends without breaking the bank, this whisky represents good value for money.

## Comparable Whiskies

When considering similar whiskies, one might look at other 21-year-old blends like the Chivas Regal 21 Year Old Royal Salute or the Ballantine's 21 Year Old. While these whiskies share an age statement, The Antiquary 21 Year Old offers a unique profile that sets it apart, perhaps leaning towards a more subtle and nuanced experience.

## Brand and History

The Antiquary brand, named after the novel by Sir Walter Scott, has a rich history dating back to the 19th century. This heritage is felt in every glass, and the 21 Year Old blend is a testament to the brand's commitment to quality and tradition. It's a whisky that doesn't rely on pomp and circumstance but instead delivers a solid, enjoyable experience steeped in history.

## Rating: 4/5

**Summary:** The Antiquary 21 Year Old is a well-crafted blend that offers a delightful array of flavors suitable for both the whisky newcomer and the seasoned enthusiast. It's a whisky that feels both special and accessible, a combination that's not always easy to find. With its reasonable price and satisfying complexity, it earns a solid 4 out of 5, making it a worthy addition to any collection.

Whether enjoyed neat, with a splash of water, or as the foundation of a sophisticated cocktail, The Antiquary 21 Year Old is sure to provide a comforting and enjoyable experience that honors the heritage of Scotch whisky.
