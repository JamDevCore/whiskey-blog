        # Balvenie 21 Year Old PortWood Finish Review

**Brand:** Balvenie  
**Age:** 21 Years  
**ABV:** 40%  
**Price:** $216.55  

Walking into the warm embrace of aged whisky, the Balvenie 21 Year Old PortWood Finish is a dram that speaks of maturity and elegance. Upon first glance, the packaging itself, with its refined and stately appearance, hints at the craftsmanship within. 

## First Impressions
As you pour this Scotch into your glass, its deep amber hue, akin to autumnal sunset, sets the stage for the experience to come. The aroma is a complex bouquet; you're greeted with a mix of ripe fruits and a subtle sweetness that's reminiscent of rich, festive desserts.

## Flavour Palette
Upon the first sip, you're met with a velvety texture and a symphony of flavors. The influence of the port casks is unmistakable, imparting a fruity and slightly spicy character that dances on the palate. There's a harmony between the notes of dried fruits, a hint of nuttiness, and the gentle warmth of oak spices. The finish is long and indulgent, leaving a pleasant, lingering taste of sweet wine and a whisper of smoke.

## Value for Money
With a price tag of over $200, the Balvenie 21 Year Old PortWood Finish is indeed a premium purchase. However, considering the two decades of craftsmanship, the additional port cask influence, and the complexity of the whisky, it stands as a reasonable investment for special occasions or for the seasoned collector. It's not an everyday dram, but rather a contemplative sipper that invites you to savor each moment.

## Similar Whiskies
In the realm of port cask-finished whiskies, you might draw parallels to the GlenDronach Port Wood or the Glenmorangie Quinta Ruban. Both offer that delightful port influence, though each with their unique distillery characteristics and at different age statements and price points.

## The Distillery and Its Craft
The Balvenie distillery, known for its dedication to traditional craftsmanship, is one of the few distilleries that still maintains its own floor maltings. This commitment to the art of whisky-making is evident in the depth and quality of the 21 Year Old PortWood Finish. The additional aging in port casks adds a layer of sophistication to the already exceptional spirit.

## Final Thoughts
The Balvenie 21 Year Old PortWood Finish is a testament to the beauty of aged Scotch and the magic of cask finishing. It appeals to both the whisky aficionado and the curious explorer, offering a journey through its layered complexity.

**Rating:** 4.5/5

**Summary:** The Balvenie 21 Year Old PortWood Finish is an exquisite example of fine Scotch whisky, with its well-balanced port cask influence and mature character. While it may be a splurge, its intricate flavor profile and the craftsmanship behind it justify the price for those special moments when only an exceptional whisky will do.
