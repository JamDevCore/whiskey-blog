        # anCnoc 12 Year Old Review

**Distillery:** Knockdhu  
**ABV:** 40%  
**Age:** 12 Years  
**Price:** £37.80

Welcome to a review of the anCnoc 12 Year Old, a Highland single malt that promises a marriage of modernity and tradition. At a comfortable price point and with a moderate ABV, it's a whisky that seems to beckon both the seasoned enthusiast and the curious newcomer.

## First Impressions

Upon first glance, the anCnoc 12 Year Old doesn't scream for attention. The packaging is understated, yet classy, with a label that hints at a contemporary touch to an age-old craft. The bottle shape is traditional, and it feels good in the hand—promising a classic Scotch experience.

## Flavour Profile

On the nose, the anCnoc 12 opens up with a bright, fruity bouquet. There's a whisper of citrus zest mingling with green apples and a hint of honey. It's inviting, not overwhelming, like a gentle handshake from an old friend.

Taking a sip, the palate is greeted with a sweet, malty character. Vanilla and toffee notes dance around, with a light spiciness that gives it a bit of an edge. The finish is medium, leaving you with a satisfying warmth and a touch of oak and just the faintest smoky whisper—an echo of its Speyside cousins.

## Value for Money

At under £40, anCnoc 12 Year Old stands as an impressive offering. It's a whisky that doesn't demand a special occasion; it's priced for enjoyment on any given day. When you consider the age statement and the balance of flavors, it's a good bang for your buck, particularly for those who appreciate a gentler dram.

## Similar Whiskies

For those looking to explore similar profiles, you might consider the Glenmorangie 10 Year Old for its approachable character, or perhaps the Dalwhinnie 15 Year Old for another example of a Highland whisky with light, floral notes.

## The Brand and the Distillery

Knockdhu Distillery, the home of anCnoc, is nestled in the Scottish Highlands and has been crafting whisky since 1894. An interesting tidbit for whisky trivia fans: the name "anCnoc" means "the hill" in Gaelic, reflecting the distillery's picturesque setting.

The distillery prides itself on combining traditional methods with a modern twist, and this is evident in the anCnoc 12 Year Old. It's a single malt that respects its heritage while not being afraid to appeal to a contemporary palate.

## Conclusion

The anCnoc 12 Year Old is a Highland single malt that offers a delightful journey through classic Scotch whisky flavors with a modern sensibility. It's easy-drinking, versatile, and priced in a way that encourages regular enjoyment rather than rare indulgence.

**Rating:** 4/5

**Summary:** The anCnoc 12 Year Old is a well-rounded, approachable whisky with a charming flavor profile and an excellent price point. It's a dram that won't disappoint, whether you're a newbie to the whisky world or a seasoned drinker looking for a reliable staple for your cabinet.
