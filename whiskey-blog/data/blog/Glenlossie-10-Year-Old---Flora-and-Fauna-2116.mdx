        # Glenlossie 10 Year Old - Flora and Fauna Review

Welcome to my review of the Glenlossie 10 Year Old from the Flora and Fauna series. This whisky has been quietly earning respect among enthusiasts, so let's dive in and see what it's all about.

## First Impressions

Upon first glance, the Glenlossie 10 Year Old presents itself with an understated charm. The packaging, part of the Flora and Fauna series, hints at a connection with nature and simplicity. The bottle design is classic and unpretentious, suggesting a focus on the spirit itself rather than flashy presentation.

## The Flavour Journey

On the nose, Glenlossie 10 Year Old greets you with a bouquet of light floral notes, complemented by a touch of fresh fruitiness and a whisper of malt. It's inviting without being overpowering, a subtle nod to its Speyside roots.

Taking the first sip, the palate is greeted with a harmonious blend of sweetness and spice. There's a honeyed character that dances neatly with hints of oak and a dash of nutmeg. The texture is pleasantly oily, coating the mouth and paving the way for a medium-length finish that leaves a satisfying warmth with just a hint of dryness.

## Value for Money

Priced at a bit over sixty bucks, the Glenlossie 10 Year Old offers a solid Speyside experience. It may not be the most complex dram you'll encounter, but it provides a reliable and enjoyable sip that won't break the bank. It's a good value for both newcomers looking to explore the region's offerings and seasoned drinkers seeking a dependable everyday whisky.

## Similar Whiskies

If you're a fan of the Glenlossie 10 Year Old, you might also enjoy other Speyside staples like the Glenfiddich 12 Year Old or the Balvenie DoubleWood 12 Year Old. Both offer that quintessential Speyside sweetness and fruitiness, though each with their own unique twists.

## The Distillery and Brand

The Glenlossie Distillery, founded in 1876, has been a relatively low-profile player in the whisky world, often overshadowed by more prominent names. However, their contribution to the Flora and Fauna series has given them a platform to showcase the quality of their spirit. The distillery is known for its traditional methods, including the use of worm tubs to condense the spirit, which is said to add complexity to the final product.

## Conclusion

The Glenlossie 10 Year Old is a Speyside whisky that offers a gentle yet satisfying experience. It's a dram that doesn't demand your full attention but rewards you with a pleasant, well-rounded flavor profile when you give it.

**Rating: 3.5/5**

**Summary:** The Glenlossie 10 Year Old - Flora and Fauna is an approachable and enjoyable Speyside single malt. It's not the most complex whisky out there, but it's well-crafted, offering good value for money. It's the kind of whisky that can be a staple in your collection, perfect for those moments when you want a reliable and comforting dram.
