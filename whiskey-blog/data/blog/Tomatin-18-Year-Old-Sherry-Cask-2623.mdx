        # Tomatin 18 Year Old Sherry Cask Review

**Distillery:** Tomatin  
**Expression:** 18 Year Old Sherry Cask  
**ABV:** 46%

Nestled in the heart of the Scottish Highlands, the Tomatin distillery has a reputation for creating some seriously smooth drams. The 18 Year Old Sherry Cask is a testament to that tradition, offering a delightful sip that's been matured in a combination of Bourbon barrels and Sherry casks.

### First Impressions

Upon first glance, the bottle exudes a sense of elegance. The deep amber hue of the whisky promises a richness that one would expect from an 18-year-old spirit. A twist of the cap, and the aroma wafts up, hinting at a complex character that beckons you to dive in.

### On the Nose

Bringing the glass to your nose, you are greeted with a bouquet of dried fruits, a whisper of nuttiness, and a subtle yet unmistakable oaky spice. It's like walking into a room where a fruitcake has just been unwrapped, with a side of almond marzipan.

### On the Palate

The first sip is a harmonious blend of flavors. The sherry influence is clear but not overpowering. It brings forward a delightful sweetness, reminiscent of raisins and figs, followed by a touch of dark chocolate. The mid-palate evolves into a spiced warmth that's comforting, not biting, with a hint of vanilla from its Bourbon barrel maturation.

### Finish

The finish is where this whisky really shines. It's long and satisfying with a slight dryness that leaves you wanting more. The oakiness comes through here, providing a pleasant structure to the lingering sweetness.

### Value for Money

At just over a hundred bucks, the Tomatin 18 Year Old Sherry Cask is a solid investment. It's not the cheapest bottle on the shelf, but the quality and complexity it delivers are more than fair for the price. For those who enjoy a sherried whisky without the hefty price tag of some older expressions, this Tomatin offers a balanced alternative.

### Comparisons

In the realm of sherried whiskies, you might compare the Tomatin 18 to the likes of GlenDronach 18 or even a Macallan 18. Each has its own distinct personality, but Tomatin stands out for its approachability and value.

### Brand and Distillery

Tomatin has been distilling since the 18th century, but it's only in recent decades that they've truly stepped into the spotlight. They're known for their commitment to quality and have been gaining well-deserved recognition for their core range and limited releases alike.

### Final Thoughts

The Tomatin 18 Year Old Sherry Cask is a whisky that's both sophisticated and accessible. It's a dram that will appeal to both newcomers and seasoned whisky drinkers, thanks to its balance of sweet sherry notes and mature complexity.
